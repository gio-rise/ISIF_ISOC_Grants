# ISIF Asia 2026 Grant Program — Research

**Date:** 2026-04-16
**Context:** Rise is applying for two ISIF Asia 2026 grants (Infrastructure USD 75K, Knowledge USD 50K). This research captures program requirements, deadlines, evaluation criteria, and strategic considerations to inform concept note preparation.

## Findings

### Funding Categories (2026 — restructured from 2025)

| Category | Max Grant | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Inclusion | USD 20,000 | Meaningful access to Internet, applications, and services |
| Knowledge | USD 50,000 | Technical capacity and/or research for Internet network operations |
| Infrastructure | USD 75,000 | Deployment, upgrade, or optimization of critical Internet infrastructure — IXPs, DNS security, routing security, caches |

**Change from 2025:** Categories were Small ($30K), Scale-up ($85K), Impact ($150K). Maximum dropped from $150K to $75K. Separate IPv6 track ($30K–$250K) appears collapsed into unified focus areas.

### Timeline

| Milestone | Date |
|---|---|
| Concept note deadline | **24 April 2026, 11:59 PM UTC+10** (9:59 PM PHT) |
| Full proposal | Invited for shortlisted concept notes (Stage 2) — date TBA |
| Project duration | Typically 12–18 months |

### Eligibility

- **For-profit companies eligible** (Rise qualifies)
- Must be legally registered in Asia Pacific (APNIC's 56-economy service area)
- Must be able to receive funds from an Australian charity
- **No co-funding required** for developing economies (Philippines qualifies)
- Voluntary co-investment (Rise's $300K PDSCN CapEx) strengthens the application

### Concept Note Sections & Word Limits

| Section | Prompt | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Introduction | Short summary of your initiative | 250 |
| Alignment | How does this align with APNIC Foundation and ISIF Asia aims? | 75 |
| Challenge | Why have you decided to undertake this project? | 100 |
| Approach | What activities will be conducted? | 100 |
| Impact | What is the anticipated impact? | 100 |
| M&E | How will you measure project outcomes? | 75 |
| Organisational Capability | What expertise does your organisation have? | 100 |

Total budget: ~800 words across all sections.

### Application Process

1. **Pre-register** on Fluxx portal (takes up to 2 business days)
2. Submit concept note through Fluxx (no email submissions)
3. Initial screening by APNIC Foundation (eligibility, viability, completeness)
4. Expert panel assessment — independent community experts score via online platform
5. Shortlisted applicants invited to full proposal

**Portal URL:** https://apnicfoundation.fluxx.io/

### Evaluation Criteria

Scored on same dimensions as concept note sections:
- **Alignment** — fit with APNIC Foundation and ISIF Asia strategic aims
- **Challenge** — clarity and significance of the problem
- **Approach** — feasibility and quality of proposed activities
- **Impact** — scale and significance of anticipated outcomes
- **M&E** — rigor of measurement approach
- **Organisational Capability** — track record and capacity to deliver

Additional factors: impact/innovation/originality prioritized; working systems/pilot projects/scalable prototypes favored; open/interoperable systems preferred.

### 2026 Focus Areas

- IPv6 deployment, digital health, essential services, inclusive access
- Environment, energy consumption, climate impacts, disaster risk mitigation
- Proof of concept, operational research, applied innovation, emerging technologies
- **Internet infrastructure strengthening and resilience** (directly relevant to Rise)

### Budget Rules

- **No percentage-based overhead** — operational costs must be embedded directly in deliverable line items
- Expenses must be directly incurred through approved activities
- Expenses must fall between approved start and end dates
