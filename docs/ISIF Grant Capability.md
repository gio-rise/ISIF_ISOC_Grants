## Title

IXperience Pro: A Structured BGP, Peering, and Routing-Security Curriculum for Philippine Provincial ISPs

---

## 1\. Introduction

IXperience Pro is a structured, cohort-based curriculum in BGP, peering, routing security, and IXP operations designed for provincial Philippine ISP engineers, the operators serving over 50 million Filipinos outside Metro Manila. It builds on IXperience, Rise's community workshop series co-delivered with APNIC and PhNOG, which across four events (Cebu, Davao, Manila, FICTAP Manila) has engaged 155 companies and 251 participants with 71% provincial coverage. 100% of surveyed participants expressed interest in future sessions. But IXperience is episodic: no curriculum spine, no cohort progression, no local trainer pipeline.

Provincial ISP engineers today lack affordable, structured training in modern network operations. Formal programs are Manila-centric. International resources, APNIC Academy, NSRC, are excellent but scarce across the region, and reliance on international trainers limits how many Philippine workshops can run each year. IXperience Pro solves both problems: it builds a Philippine-localised curriculum with cohort-based delivery on a live production IXP, and it develops local Filipino trainers through a train-the-trainer track so the program scales independently of international instructor availability.

Over 12 months we will co-develop a four-module open-source program, BGP fundamentals, peering and interconnection, routing security (RPKI/MANRS), and IXP-operator essentials, with APNIC Academy instructors, incorporating a train-the-trainer component that certifies 4–6 Filipino engineers as future workshop facilitators. Four 3-day cohorts of 15 engineers (60 total) from at least 20 provincial ISPs train in rotating cities using GetaFIX as a live peering lab. Graduates join an alumni network anchored by Rise's MOA with FICTAP. All materials publish under CC-BY. This proposal is self-contained and fully separable from any infrastructure application.

---

## 2\. Alignment

This proposal advances ISIF Asia 2026's Capability priority by building hands-on proficiency in BGP, peering, routing security (RPKI/MANRS), and IXP operations among provincial Philippine ISP engineers, practitioners underserved by existing training pathways across Asia Pacific. The cohort-based curriculum complements APNIC Academy offerings with localised, field-delivered instruction and produces durable open-source materials reusable across APAC economies. It targets the operators most at risk of widening skills disparity.

---

## 3\. Challenge

Provincial ISP engineers across the Philippines have no structured, affordable pathway into modern network-operations practice. Many provincial operators emerged from cable television, now running fiber without formal networking staff, learning BGP, RPKI, and peering on the job. Episodic workshops teach skills that decay without reinforcement. No Philippine program offers cohort-based engineering training, no alumni community connects mid-career provincial engineers, and no localised curriculum addresses their actual starting point. The capability gap compounds: under-peered networks cost more to operate, routing security goes undeployed, and expertise concentrates in Manila while provincial subscribers absorb the consequences through higher prices and lower performance.

---

## 4\. Approach

We will: 

(1) co-develop an open-source four-module curriculum, BGP fundamentals, peering and interconnection, routing security (RPKI/MANRS), IXP-operator practice, with APNIC Academy instructors, localised to Philippine equipment, regulation, and peering topology; 

(2) run a train-the-trainer track: 4–6 Filipino engineers shadow APNIC-led early cohorts, co-deliver mid-program, and lead independently by cohort 4, earning facilitator certification; 

(3) deliver four 3-day cohorts of 15 engineers in provincial cities, with GetaFIX as a live peering lab, later cohorts and any concurrent provincial workshops serve as real-world practicum for trainer candidates; 

(4) graduate participants through hands-on assessments with public certificates and alumni access anchored by the Rise-FICTAP MOA; (5) release all materials under CC-BY.

---

## 5\. Impact

Within 12 months: 60 certified provincial ISP engineers from at least 20 ISPs spanning Visayas, Mindanao, and Luzon; 4–6 certified Filipino trainers capable of delivering future cohorts independently; an open-source, Philippine-localised curriculum freely available to every APAC training provider; and measurable gains in RPKI deployment, MANRS compliance, and new peering sessions among participating ISPs, tracked against public baselines. Beyond the grant period, local trainers deliver future cohorts at marginal cost without international instructor dependency, alumni engagement is community-sustained through the FICTAP partnership, and the program shifts network-engineering expertise decisively outward from Manila.

---

## 6\. M\&E

Four published indicators: 

(1) cohort completion rate (target 80%+); 

(2) statistically significant pre/post-assessment gains per module; 

(3) six-month post-graduation survey measuring on-the-job skill application, ROA publication, MANRS compliance, new peering sessions established, serving as the headline outcome metric; 

(4) alumni-network engagement via quarterly meetup attendance and peer-support activity. An independent APNIC Academy reviewer evaluates curriculum quality before the second cohort. All results publish quarterly on an open project page.
