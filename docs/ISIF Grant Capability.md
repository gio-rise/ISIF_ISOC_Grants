### 1. Introduction (250 words)

IXperience Pro is a structured, cohort-based curriculum in BGP, peering, routing security, and IXP operations for provincial Philippine ISP engineers, the operators serving over 50 million Filipinos outside Metro Manila. It builds on IXperience, RISE's community workshop series co-delivered with APNIC and PhNOG, which across four events has engaged 155 companies and 251 participants with 71% provincial coverage and 100% participant interest in future sessions. But IXperience is episodic: no curriculum spine, no cohort progression, no local trainer pipeline.

Provincial ISP engineers lack affordable, structured training in modern network operations. Formal programs are Manila-centric. APNIC Academy and NSRC are excellent but scarce, and reliance on international instructors caps how many Philippine workshops can run each year. IXperience Pro solves both problems: a Philippine-localised curriculum delivered on a live production IXP, and a train-the-trainer track that develops local Filipino facilitators so the program scales independently of international instructors.

Over 12 months we will co-develop a four-module open-source program, BGP fundamentals, peering and interconnection, routing security (RPKI/MANRS), and IXP-operator essentials, with APNIC Academy, certifying 4–6 Filipino engineers as future facilitators. Four 3-day cohorts of 15 engineers (60 total) from at least 20 provincial ISPs train in rotating cities using GetaFIX as a live peering lab. Graduates join an alumni network anchored by RISE's MOA with FICTAP. All materials publish under CC-BY.

*(219 words)*

### 2. Alignment (75 words)

This proposal advances ISIF Asia 2026 strategic aims by building hands-on proficiency in BGP, peering, routing security (RPKI/MANRS), and IXP operations among provincial Philippine ISP engineers, practitioners underserved by existing training pathways across Asia Pacific. It directly serves the Expanding Connectivity and Capability theme and strengthens Internet infrastructure resilience through RPKI/MANRS adoption. The cohort-based curriculum complements APNIC Academy offerings with localised, field-delivered instruction and produces durable open-source materials reusable across APAC economies.

*(72 words)*

---

### 3. Challenge (100 words)

Provincial ISP engineers across the Philippines have no structured, affordable pathway into modern network-operations practice. Many provincial operators emerged from cable television, now running fiber without formal networking staff, learning BGP, RPKI, and peering on the job. Episodic workshops teach skills that decay without reinforcement. No Philippine program offers cohort-based engineering training, no alumni community connects mid-career provincial engineers, and no localised curriculum addresses their actual starting point. The capability gap compounds: under-peered networks cost more to operate, routing security goes undeployed, and expertise concentrates in Manila while provincial subscribers absorb the consequences through higher prices and lower performance.

*(99 words)*

---

### 4. Approach (100 words, bulleted)

- Co-develop open-source four-module curriculum with APNIC Academy: BGP fundamentals, peering and interconnection, routing security (RPKI/MANRS), IXP-operator practice; localised to Philippine equipment, regulation, and peering topology
- Run train-the-trainer track: 4–6 Filipino engineers shadow early cohorts, co-deliver mid-program, lead cohort 4 independently, earning facilitator certification
- Deliver four 3-day cohorts of 15 engineers (60 total) in provincial cities with GetaFIX as live peering lab
- Graduate participants through hands-on assessments with public certificates and FICTAP-anchored alumni network
- Release all materials under CC-BY

*(78 words)*

---

### 5. Impact (100 words)

Within 12 months: 60 certified provincial ISP engineers from at least 20 ISPs spanning Visayas, Mindanao, and Luzon; 4–6 certified Filipino trainers capable of delivering future cohorts independently; an open-source, Philippine-localised curriculum freely available to every APAC training provider; and measurable gains in RPKI deployment, MANRS compliance, and new peering sessions among participating ISPs, tracked against public baselines. Beyond the grant period, local trainers deliver future cohorts at marginal cost without international instructor dependency, alumni engagement is community-sustained through the FICTAP partnership, and the program shifts network-engineering expertise decisively outward from Manila.

*(92 words)*

---

### 6. M&E (75 words)

Four published indicators:

(1) cohort completion rate (target 80%+);

(2) statistically significant pre/post-assessment gains per module;

(3) six-month post-graduation survey measuring on-the-job skill application, ROA publication, MANRS compliance, new peering sessions established, serving as the headline outcome metric;

(4) alumni-network engagement via quarterly meetup attendance and peer-support activity. An independent APNIC Academy reviewer evaluates curriculum quality before the second cohort. All results publish quarterly on an open project page.

*(65 words)*

### 7. Organisational Capability (100 words)

RISE operates GetaFIX, the Philippines' largest neutral multi-city internet exchange (Manila, Cebu, Davao), with 126+ Manila members including Meta, Google, Amazon, Cloudflare, and major carriers, peak traffic exceeding 1 Tbps, and 1,952 networks connected across 11 data centers. IXperience has engaged 155 companies and 251 participants with 71% provincial coverage. RISE is FICTAP's capability partner under standing MOA. Prior grants: 2023 ISIF Asia (Project School Internet, 50+ schools, contributing to 100,000+ students via RISE School Grants); 2024 USTDA award (~USD 1.27M) for GetaFIX underserved-area expansion. Core team includes Gio Bacareza (ex-Smart VP), Dylan Clear (ex-Juniper), Robertas Zudys (ex-Telia).
