## Project Title

IXPerience Philippines 2026: Regional Peering-Community Workshops Seeded by GetaFIX

---

## 1\. Project Summary

RISE proposes "IXPerience Philippines 2026", six regional peering-community workshops across the Philippine provinces, designed to bring the peering ecosystem to the several hundred ISPs operating outside Metro Manila. If awarded, this would be the first SPI grant in the Philippines, and only the second in Southeast Asia, following the Myanmar Internet Exchange Association (MMIX) in 2024.

RISE implemented IXperience 2025, a technical workshop series co-produced with DE-CIX, APNIC and PhNOG. Across four events (Cebu, Davao, Manila, and FICTAP Manila), IXperience 2025 has reached 155 companies and 251 participants, with 71% provincial attendance. In post-event surveys, 100% of participants expressed interest in future sessions; 67% rated the experience as "loved the event."

The IXPerience Philippines 2026 technical workshops will be in six new provincial cities, local chapter formation with identified champions, alumni community infrastructure (platform, recordings, captioning), and systematic documentation of community interest in domestic peering.

The six SPI-funded workshops will be held in Cebu (July 2026, co-located with a PhNOG event), Cagayan de Oro (the Mindanao hub), Iloilo, Clark (Pampanga), Naga (Camarines Sur), and Tacloban (Leyte). These cities were selected as the most concentrated provincial-ISP hubs across six regions: Central Visayas, Central Luzon, Eastern Visayas, Bicol, Western Visayas including Negros, and all of Mindanao. Precise ISP counts are hard to pin down, as there is no single authoritative Philippine registry and public sources (NTC, PeeringDB, CAIDA, FICTAP and PCTA rosters) each capture a different slice; but best-available estimates place the total across these regions at several hundred operators, representing the bulk of provincial connectivity demand outside Metro Manila.

Participation is open to any ISP, content network, or enterprise regardless of IXP affiliation. Workshop outcomes are measured by new peering relationships established at any Philippine IXP, not exclusively at GetaFIX. RISE co-invests approximately USD 25,000 in staff time and partner contributions alongside this grant.

## 2\. Problem Statement

The Philippine peering ecosystem suffers from a structural geographic gap. Peering expertise, community events, IXP access, and mentorship concentrate almost entirely in Metro Manila, while the majority of Philippine ISPs operate in the provinces. Precise counts are hard to come by: there is no single authoritative Philippine ISP registry, and public sources (NTC licensees, PeeringDB, CAIDA, FICTAP and PCTA rosters) each capture a different slice. Best-available estimates put the figure at roughly 1,500 ISPs nationally, with around two-thirds operating outside Metro Manila. Most Philippine internet traffic still routes through international hubs in Singapore, Hong Kong, and Tokyo, even though neutral IXP presence now exists in Cebu and Davao.

The operational reality compounds the geography. A large share of provincial ISPs grew out of cable television: DOCSIS-era operators now transitioning to fiber broadband, often without formal network-engineering staff. They are being asked to learn BGP, peering economics, RPKI, and IXP operations on the job, while simultaneously running a fiber build. This is why IXperience 2025 drew 155 companies across four events; the demand for structured exposure to internet networking from provincial operators is pent-up, not hypothetical.

Provincial ISPs buy transit where they could be peering. They rely on long, Manila-centric paths when shorter regional paths exist. Most have never attended a peering forum, never seen a route-server configuration walked through live, and have no relationship with the peering community that coordinates routing hygiene, IXP growth, and industry advocacy.

This is not a technology problem. GetaFIX operates in three cities. The barrier is community distance: provincial operators are disconnected from the knowledge, relationships, and norms that make peering work. The Konektadong Pinoy Act is set to bring a new wave of small ISPs into the Philippine market, many drawn from the same cable-TV-to-fiber cohort, exactly the operators who need structured introduction to the peering ecosystem before they default to single-carrier transit dependency.

## 3\. Proposed Activities

**Activity 1: Six regional peering-community workshops (Months 1–10)**

Six 1 to 3-day workshops in provincial cities, each hosting 25–40 participants from ISPs, content networks, academic networks, and enterprise operators in the region:

- Cebu (July 2026, co-located with PhNOG event), serving Central Visayas (86 ISPs)  
- Cagayan de Oro, serving all of Mindanao (182 ISPs across 6 regions)  
- Iloilo, serving Western Visayas and Negros Island (107 ISPs)  
- Clark, Pampanga, serving Central Luzon (131 ISPs)  
- Naga, Camarines Sur, serving Bicol Region (87 ISPs)  
- Tacloban, Leyte, serving Eastern Visayas (92 ISPs)

Each workshop should cover technical track (peering fundamentals, route-server configuration, RPKI, MANRS readiness, and others) and a community track (peering economics, transit optimization, community interest documentation, local chapter formation). Curriculum is vendor-neutral and IXP-neutral, no content is specific to any single IXP operator.

Early workshops are delivered by RISE engineers alongside APNIC and PhNOG facilitators, the same program model that delivered IXperience 2025. RISE is developing a local Filipino trainer pipeline through a parallel ISIF Asia Capability application; if that grant is awarded, later SPI workshops serve as real-world practicum for trainer candidates, progressively shifting delivery from international to local facilitators. Capability is an accelerator, not a prerequisite: SPI ships on the proven IXperience 2025 delivery model regardless of Capability's outcome. FICTAP, RISE's designated capability and capacity improvement partner under a standing MOA, supports outreach and participant recruitment through its national ISP membership.

Participation is free and open. Attendance is not conditional on joining any IXP.

**Activity 2: Community infrastructure build-out (Months 1–12)**

- Alumni platform (mailing list or online community) connecting all workshop participants across regions  
- Video recordings with captioning for all workshop sessions, published under CC-BY license  
- Community interest documentation: each workshop produces a summary of local peering demand, existing connectivity patterns, and barriers to peering, shared publicly to inform future IXP planning by any operator

**Activity 3: Regional chapter seeding (Months 3–12)**

Each workshop identifies a local champion (a provincial ISP engineer or operator) to lead a continuing quarterly virtual meetup for the region. After 12 months, chapters operate independently or affiliate with PhNOG's regional structure.

**Governance and conflict-of-interest safeguards:**

- A steering committee comprising PhNOG and APNIC representatives provides independent oversight of SPI fund use  
- SPI grant accounting is maintained separately from RISE's commercial IXP operations  
- All outcome metrics track peering at any Philippine IXP, not exclusively GetaFIX  
- Workshop curriculum, participant data, and community interest documentation are available to any Philippine IXP operator on equal terms

## 4\. Target Outcomes and Indicators

**Reach:**

- 150–240 provincial ISP engineers and operators participate across six workshops (baseline: IXperience averaged 40+ participants per event in 2025–2026)  
- At least 80 unique companies represented, with 70%+ from outside Metro Manila

**Peering ecosystem growth (IXP-neutral):**

- At least 20 new peering relationships established by workshop alumni at any Philippine IXP within 12 months of attendance, tracked via PeeringDB and self-reported alumni surveys, not limited to any single IXP's member roster  
- Increase in MANRS participation among workshop alumni (baseline measured at registration; follow-up at 6 months)

**Community durability:**

- 6 regional peering chapters seeded, each with an identified local champion  
- At least 4 of 6 chapters hold a follow-up meetup (virtual or in-person) within 6 months of the workshop, without RISE staff facilitation  
- Alumni community platform with 200+ subscribers and documented quarterly activity

**Knowledge commons:**

- Full curriculum (slides, labs, recordings) published under CC-BY license and downloaded or reused by at least 3 organizations outside RISE within 12 months  
- 6 community interest reports (one per city) published and accessible to any IXP operator

**Survey feedback:**

- Post-workshop satisfaction: target 90%+ "would recommend" (baseline from prior IXperience: 100% expressed interest in future sessions, 67% "loved the event")

**Layered funding and premium uplift:**

SPI's USD 25,000 covers the lean execution of all six legs plus essential cross-program overhead, with per-leg SPI allocations running roughly $500 below the marketing-recommended lean per-city costs. The balance is closed by:

- Operator and carrier sponsorships per leg (target partners: Globe, PLDT, Converge, GreenIX, DE-CIX)
- RISE operational budget for strategic-city uplift (Clark and Cagayan de Oro, where premium execution better serves the Konektadong Pinoy cohort)
- PhNOG Conference 2026 absorption of the Cebu leg's direct event costs

If premium-level execution proves warranted in Clark and/or Cagayan de Oro, the incremental $1,500 to $3,000 per city is fully sponsor-funded or RISE-funded and does not draw on SPI.

**What SPI funds vs. what RISE funds:**

The curriculum and courseware already exist; APNIC Academy provides online labs and training materials at no cost, and IXperience has two years of proven workshop content. The constraint preventing expansion into provincial cities is not content; it is logistics. Venue, meals, AV, facilitator travel, and networking socials account for the bulk of every workshop's cost. SPI removes this constraint for five net-new provincial cities that RISE cannot unilaterally underwrite at the quality bar reviewers would expect.

**RISE co-investment:** RISE contributes staff time for program coordination, logistics planning, and FICTAP partnership management, valued at approximately USD 25,000 across the program year, plus strategic-city operational top-ups where sponsorship falls short. IXperience 2025 technical workshops in Cebu, Davao, and Manila were delivered entirely from RISE's operational budget; SPI is requested for the specific expansion into five provincial cities that would not otherwise be funded.

## 6\. Why RISE

RISE operates GetaFIX (AS23942), the Philippines' only neutral, open Internet Exchange Point, with presence in Manila, Cebu, and Davao. Since launching in 2018 with 5 founding members, GetaFIX has grown to 126+ Manila members, including Meta, Google, Amazon, TikTok, Cloudflare, Akamai, and major Philippine carriers, with peak traffic exceeding 1 Tbps. It is the country's largest multi-city neutral IXP.

RISE delivered IXperience 2025 alongside program partners APNIC, PhNOG and DE-CIX, with community outreach through FICTAP (under a standing MOA designating RISE as FICTAP's capability and capacity improvement partner) and PCTA. Across four events to date (Cebu, Davao, Manila, FICTAP Manila), IXperience has reached 155 unique companies and 251 participants, with 71% provincial attendance.

RISE has previously managed international grant-funded projects at scale. In 2023, RISE received a USD 150,000 ISIF Asia grant (Project School Internet), connecting 50+ schools and reaching approximately 80,000 students across Metro Cebu, with RISE co-investing USD 130,000 in services. In May 2024, the U.S. Trade and Development Agency awarded approximately USD 1.27 million for a GetaFIX expansion feasibility study, signed at the 6th Indo-Pacific Business Forum.

**For-profit alignment as a sustainability advantage:** As a commercial IXP operator, RISE has a durable business incentive to maintain the peering community beyond any grant cycle. The program's success directly strengthens the ecosystem RISE serves, the same model that sustains LINX and AMS-IX as commercial operators that invest in community building because a healthy peering ecosystem is their core business. This alignment means SPI's investment is not at risk of evaporating when grant funding ends.

## 7\. Sustainability

The program is designed to persist beyond the SPI-funded year through three mechanisms:

**Commercial alignment.** RISE allocates staff time and sponsorship revenue (approximately USD 25,000 per year) to continue at least two peering-community workshops annually after the grant period. As a commercial IXP operator, RISE's business model depends on a growing, healthy peering ecosystem, this is not philanthropic overhead but a core operational investment. The same commercial logic sustains community programs at LINX and AMS-IX globally.

**Institutional partnerships.** RISE's standing MOA with FICTAP ensures ongoing access to FICTAP's national ISP membership for outreach, recruitment, and event coordination. PhNOG provides the technical community anchor. These relationships do not depend on grant funding.

**Reusable assets and local trainer pipeline.** All curriculum materials, workshop recordings, and community interest reports are published under CC-BY license and freely available to any organization. RISE is separately developing a train-the-trainer program (under an ISIF Asia Capability application) to certify 4–6 local Filipino facilitators. By Year 2, these trainers can deliver provincial peering workshops independently, no international flights, no APNIC scheduling constraints, marginal cost limited to venue and food. This is the key to scaling beyond the 6 cities funded by SPI: once local trainers are certified, expanding to additional provinces costs approximately $2,500 per event. The alumni community platform and regional chapter structure are community-moderated and designed to operate without RISE staff facilitation after the initial 12-month support period.

**Post-grant trajectory.** By month 12, we expect at least four of six regional chapters to be self-sustaining, with local champions running quarterly meetups independently. RISE continues to provide platform hosting and remote advisory support, funded from commercial operations. If additional grant funding becomes available through future SPI, ISIF Asia, or ICANN rounds, RISE will apply to expand into additional provincial cities, but the core community infrastructure seeded by this grant does not require continued external funding to survive.
