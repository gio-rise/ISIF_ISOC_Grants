Subject: Request for Letter of Support — ISOC Sustainable Peering Infrastructure Grant Application

Dear FICTAP Leadership,

I hope this message finds you well.

Rise is preparing an application to the Internet Society Foundation's Sustainable Peering Infrastructure (SPI) Grant Program for 2026. The application is for the Peering Ecosystem Support track (up to USD 25,000) and proposes a series of regional peering-community workshops across the Philippines, building on our existing IXperience program.

As you know, Rise and FICTAP have a standing Memorandum of Agreement designating Rise as FICTAP's capability and capacity improvement partner. Under this MOA, we delivered an IXperience workshop at the FICTAP Conference in Manila this past March 2026, reaching 57 attendees from 25 companies. The SPI-funded project would expand this collaboration by adding structured peering-community meetups in provincial cities where FICTAP members operate, helping independent cable operators and ISPs access affordable peering and strengthen their network operations.

We would be grateful if FICTAP could provide a brief letter of support for our application. The letter does not commit FICTAP to any additional financial obligation beyond our existing MOA — it simply confirms:

1. The existing MOA between FICTAP and Rise designating Rise as FICTAP's capability/capacity improvement partner
2. That FICTAP members (independent cable TV operators and ISPs) would benefit from regional peering-community workshops and IXP onboarding outside Metro Manila
3. FICTAP's endorsement of the proposed SPI project as aligned with the goals of our partnership
